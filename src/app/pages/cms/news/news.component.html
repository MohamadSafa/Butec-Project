Sharjah Waste to Energy Company

Masdar and Bee’ah jointly developed the first Waste-to-Energy plant in the United Arab Emirates. The plant’s power generation capacity is circa 30MW and aims to divert more than 300,000 tonnes of solid waste from landfill each year. This project is the first of its kind in the UAE. The waste incineration process converts waste into heat which is then used to produce steam and which in turn is used to drive a turbine and generator block. On the other hand, there are 2 main by-products of the process: (i) bottom ash which is fully recovered and transported using into a safe storage area before being exported for use by other industries (ii) flue gas which after being treated by a cutting-edge treatment process is released into the air free from any element that could harm the environment. In summary, this plant is impressive due to its ability to transform waste that would normally emit methane and contribute to global warming, into an energy-producing fuel that frees up land, generates power, and minimizes carbon emissions all while creating a usable by-product. BUTEC executed the civil works of the entire site and the construction of all buildings
